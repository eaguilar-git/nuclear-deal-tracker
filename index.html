Methodology
Sum of Capital Value (low) across all deals in the current filter, normalized to USD using approximate FX rates at time of announcement. Includes all deal types — PPA contract values, equity investments, grants, and loans. Use the Significance and Deal Type filters to narrow to specific capital categories (e.g. filter to Financing for committed equity/debt/grants only). Values marked "Up to" or "At least" use the stated figure as the lower bound.
Capacity Tracked ?
—
GW across linked projects
Methodology
Sum of Total Capacity from all projects linked to deals in the current filter, deduplicated by project (each project counted once). Values entered in MW or TW are normalized to GW. Reflects planned or operational capacity — not necessarily online today.

What is a Program?
A Program is a high-level initiative that may span multiple sites or developers. Examples include a company's overall nuclear strategy (e.g. Amazon Nuclear Initiative), a multi-reactor effort at one site (e.g. Project Matador), or a government umbrella (e.g. DOE ARDP). Programs contain one or many Projects.

—
across all programs
What is a Project?
A Project is a specific reactor development or commercial agreement tied to a named site and lead company — e.g. "Microsoft – Constellation (TMI-1)" or "Dow – X-energy (Seadrift SMR)". Each project has one primary lead and belongs to a parent program. Projects may contain multiple reactor units.
Reactors ?
—
units tracked
What is a Reactor?
A Reactor is an individual reactor unit within a project — e.g. "Unit 1 (AP1000)" or "Pioneer Unit 1 (SMR-300)". Only multi-unit projects have separate reactor rows. Single-unit projects are tracked at the project level. Reactor-level deals are those tied to a specific unit rather than the project as a whole.
